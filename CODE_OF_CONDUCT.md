# Contributor Code of Conduct:

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the **LLM-Lab** project pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of technical experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and technically healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive and collaborative environment include:

* Demonstrating empathy, patience, and kindness toward other project members.
* Being respectful of differing opinions, infrastructure approaches, and proposed automation architectures.
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback in a professional manner during code reviews or issue analysis.
* Focusing on what is best for the long-term stability of the lab ecosystem.
* Recognizing and valuing the time and effort invested by open-source developers.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
* Trolling, personal insults, technical snobbery, or ad hominem attacks.
* Public or private harassment in any of the repository's communication channels (Issues, Pull Requests, or discussion threads).
* Publishing others' private information without their explicit permission (such as API tokens, passwords, public IP addresses, or private correspondence).
* Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional technical setting.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate, fair, and proportionate corrective action in response to any instances of behavior that do not align with this Code of Conduct. This includes the right and responsibility to edit, reject, or remove comments, commits, code, documentation, or issues that violate this document.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project community spaces, and also applies in public spaces when an individual is officially representing **LLM-Lab** or **Tecno Consultores**.

## Enforcement and Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported confidentially and directly to the repository administrators. All alerts will be reviewed carefully and investigated, maintaining the confidentiality of the affected person and the reporter of the incident.
