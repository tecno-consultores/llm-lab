# Contributor Code of Conduct[cite: 5]

## Our Pledge[cite: 5]

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the **LLM-Lab** project pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of technical experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.[cite: 5]

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and technically healthy community.[cite: 5]

## Our Standards[cite: 5]

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive and collaborative environment include:[cite: 5]

* Demonstrating empathy, patience, and kindness toward other project members.[cite: 5]
* Being respectful of differing opinions, infrastructure approaches, and proposed automation architectures.[cite: 5]
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback in a professional manner during code reviews or issue analysis.[cite: 5]
* Focusing on what is best for the long-term stability of the lab ecosystem.[cite: 5]
* Recognizing and valuing the time and effort invested by open-source developers.[cite: 5]

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:[cite: 5]

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.[cite: 5]
* Trolling, personal insults, technical snobbery, or ad hominem attacks.[cite: 5]
* Public or private harassment in any of the repository's communication channels (Issues, Pull Requests, or discussion threads).[cite: 5]
* Publishing others' private information without their explicit permission (such as API tokens, passwords, public IP addresses, or private correspondence).[cite: 5]
* Any conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional technical setting.[cite: 5]

## Enforcement Responsibilities[cite: 5]

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior.[cite: 5] They will take appropriate, fair, and proportionate corrective action in response to any instances of behavior that do not align with this Code of Conduct.[cite: 5] This includes the right and responsibility to edit, reject, or remove comments, commits, code, documentation, or issues that violate this document.[cite: 5]

## Scope[cite: 5]

This Code of Conduct applies within all project community spaces, and also applies in public spaces when an individual is officially representing **LLM-Lab** or **Tecno Consultores**.[cite: 5]

## Enforcement and Reporting[cite: 5]

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported confidentially and directly to the repository administrators.[cite: 5] All alerts will be reviewed carefully and investigated, maintaining the confidentiality of the affected person and the reporter of the incident.[cite: 5]
